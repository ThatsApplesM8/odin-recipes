Potato Salad

Completed potato salad mixed with potaoesm milk, custard, and other additions.

The recipe is supposedly the best-ever potato salad. The recipe has the creamiest, richest potato base along with the homemade salad dressing adding along with the potatoes.

The simple, yet delicate dish is light, containing only 555 calories with 46g in Fat, 27g in Carbs, and 11g in Protein. This makes it great for parties with solid appetizers.

Ingredients

  • 6 medium potatoes
  • 1/2 cup white sugar
  • 2 large eggsm beaten
  • salt to taste
  • 1 (5 ounce) can evaporated milk
  • 1.2 cup vingear
  • 1 teaspoon prepared yellow mustard
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 cup mayonaise
  • 6 large hard-cooked eggs, diced
  • 1 cup celery, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Directions

  1. Place potatoes in a large pot and cover with salted water; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, about 20 minutes. Drain and set aside to cool.
  2. While the potatoes are cooking, whisk together sugar, 2 beaten eggs, and cornstarch in a saucepan; season with salt.
  3. Stir in milk, vinegar, and mustard. Cook and stir over medium heat until thickened, about 10 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in butter. Refrigerate until cool, then stir in mayonnaise.
  4. Peel and fice potatoes. Transfer to a large bowl; toss with hard-cooked eggs, onion, celery, and 1 teaspoon salt.
  5. Stir dressing gently into the bowl of potato salad until evenly coated.
  6. CHill before serving, 3 hours to overnight.
